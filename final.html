A Popular Breakfast Food is no Longer Healthy

The U.S Food and Drug Administration updated the guidelines for Nutrition Facts label and changed the criteria for what's "healthy."
By Sarah Russo

According to the updated standards from the FDA, a healthy stamp would be given to cereals that have three-fourth ounces of whole grains and no more than 1 gram of saturated fat, 230 milligrams of sodium and 2.5 grams of added sugars.
